This Privacy Policy explains how Thoi Nguyen ("we", "us", or "our") collects, uses, and discloses information about you when you use our mobile application "Chúc Tết 2026" (the "App").

By using the App, you agree to the collection and use of information in accordance with this policy.

1. Information We Collect

Privacy First: We do not collect, store, or share your personal photos, contacts, or precise location data. All greeting card customization happens locally on your device.

We do not require you to create an account to use the App. We do not collect personally identifiable information such as your name, email address, phone number, or home address.

To help us improve the App and fix bugs, we use Google Firebase services. These services may collect:

  • Device Information (e.g., iPhone model, iOS version).
  • App Usage Data (e.g., how long the app is used, which screens are visited).
  • Crash Reports (e.g., stack traces when the app closes unexpectedly).
  • Installation UUIDs (randomly generated IDs to identify a unique installation, not the user).

2. Third-Party Services

We use the following third-party services to ensure the App runs smoothly:

Google Firebase (Analytics & Crashlytics)
We use Firebase to analyze user behavior and report crashes. This data is aggregated and anonymized. Firebase does not provide us with your personal identity.
For more information, please visit the Google Privacy Policy.

3. How We Use Information

We use the collected data strictly for the following purposes:

  • To provide and maintain the App.
  • To monitor the usage of the App (via Firebase Analytics) to understand which features are popular.
  • To detect, prevent, and address technical issues (via Firebase Crashlytics).

4. Data Security

The security of your data is important to us. Since the App does not store personal data on external servers (other than anonymous analytics data stored by Google), the risk of personal data breach is minimal.

Our App is designed for general audiences, including families. We do not knowingly collect personally identifiable information from children under the age of 13.
