2. Third-Party Services

We use the following services from Google Firebase to improve app stability and analyze usage trends:

  • Firebase Analytics: We use this to understand how users interact with the app (e.g., which Tet greetings are most popular, which recipes are viewed) to improve the content. Data is collected anonymously.
  • Firebase Crashlytics: We use this to detect and fix crashes. If the app closes unexpectedly, anonymous logs (including device type, OS version, and stack trace) are sent to us to help fix the bug.

Links to the privacy policy of third-party service providers used by the app:

3. Device Permissions

To provide specific features such as creating greeting cards and saving them, the App requires access to your device's photo library. We are transparent about why we need these permissions:

iOS Permission: Privacy - Photo Library Usage Description

This permission is required for the "Create New Card" and "Edit Template" features. We access your photo library only when you explicitly choose to upload a personal photo to insert into a Tet greeting card frame.

iOS Permission: Privacy - Photo Library Additions Usage Description

This permission is required to save your finished greeting cards to your device. When you finish editing a card or choose to download a template, the app needs permission to write this image file to your Camera Roll so you can share it later.

Note: We do not upload your photos to any private server. The processing of images happens locally on your device.

4. App Features and Data

The App provides various utility features related to the Lunar New Year (Tet):

  • Sharing Greetings: When you share an image or copy a text message, this action is handled by your device's native sharing capabilities.
  • Lucky Wheel: The content you set for the Lucky Wheel is stored locally on your device to remember your preferences.
  • Tet Information (Taboos, Customs, Recipes): This content is read-only and does not collect user data.
